# Voice: Engagement Layer (Layer 3)

Layer 3 of the three-layer voice system. Loaded together with `do-not.md`
(Layer 1) and `personal.md` (Layer 2) when output is engagement-facing.

This file is populated by the `engagement-bootstrap-from-urls` skill from
URLs the engagement provides (brand site, style guide, sample artifacts).
Until then, sections stay empty and the system falls back to Layers 1 + 2.

When populated, fill each section with concrete patterns observed in the
engagement's existing content. Cite the URL or document the pattern came
from so future readers can verify.

## § Voice

Adjectives that fit the engagement's brand voice, plus adjectives that
don't. Pull from the brand's existing copy if a style guide is unavailable.

## § Sentence shape

Mean and median sentence length. Distribution of short, medium, long.
Any signature rhythms worth replicating.

## § Hedging + conviction

How the engagement signals confidence. How it softens. How it apologizes
when something breaks. Rate per 1,000 words where measurable.

## § Sign-offs

Default sign-off in email. Variants by recipient type. What the engagement
never closes with.

## § Common vocabulary

Words and phrases the engagement reaches for. Words it avoids. House terms
for products, features, customer segments.

## § Punctuation and mechanics

Exclamation rate. Em dashes (banned per Layer 1 regardless). Contractions.
Curly quotes vs. straight. Emoji rules per format.

## § Format-specific tendencies

### Email

Greeting pattern, opener pattern, body length, close pattern.

### Slack

Greeting use, threading conventions, emoji conventions, sign-off rules.

### Doc

Concise variant vs. detailed variant tendencies. TL;DR norms. Heading
density. Citation expectations.

### Ticket

Title format. Acceptance criteria conventions. Sizing conventions.

### Presentation

Slide title style. Body density. Chart vs. text preference. Closing slide
norms.

## § What this engagement avoids naturally

Patterns the engagement's own writers don't use, so Claude shouldn't
reach for them even when other layers would permit them.

## § Application notes

How this layer composes with Layers 1 + 2. Any overrides where the
engagement's voice conflicts with the personal baseline.
